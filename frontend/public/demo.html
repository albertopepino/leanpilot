A3: Reduce CNC Scrap Rate by 50%
Owner: Marco Rossi · 2026-03-01
1. Background

CNC Line A scrap rate has increased from 2.1% to 4.8% over the past quarter, resulting in approximately EUR 12,000/month in wasted material and rework costs. Customer complaints have risen 40%.

2. Current Condition

Average scrap rate: 4.8%. Primary defects: dimensional out-of-tolerance (62%), surface finish (23%), burrs (15%). CNC #3 accounts for 70% of scrap. No tool wear monitoring in place.

3. Goal / Target

Reduce scrap rate from 4.8% to 2.4% (50% reduction) within 60 days. Target: save EUR 6,000/month in material waste. Zero customer complaints related to dimensional accuracy.

4. Root Cause Analysis

5-WHY analysis revealed: tool wear monitoring absent, no preventive replacement schedule, quality cost data not linked to CAPEX decisions. Pareto shows 62% of defects are dimensional — directly linked to tool condition.

5. Countermeasures

1) Install tool wear sensor on CNC #3 (Week 1-2). 2) Create tool replacement schedule based on cut count (Week 2). 3) Implement in-process SPC checks every 25 parts (Week 3). 4) Train operators on new monitoring dashboard (Week 3-4).

6. Implementation Plan

Week 1-2: Sensor procurement and installation. Week 3: SPC setup and operator training. Week 4: Go-live with full monitoring. Week 5-8: Monitor results, fine-tune thresholds. Budget: EUR 3,500 for sensor + EUR 500 training.

7. Follow-up

Weekly scrap rate review in production meeting. 30-day checkpoint: target 3.5% scrap rate. 60-day checkpoint: target 2.4%. Monthly quality cost report to management. Success criteria: sustained scrap < 2.5% for 4 consecutive weeks.
